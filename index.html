How I lead

What excites me as an experience design lead.

Six things I find genuinely energizing about leading experience design — building the teams behind the work, shipping the AI tooling, and designing for every actor in the ecosystem, not just the user on the screen.

01

Bridge service and product.

Service design tells you what to build and why. Product design tells you whether it'll actually land in someone's hand. I do both — and the gap between the two is where most experience failures live.

02

Build the team that builds the team.

Inside the org: embedding service design teams that keep scaling after I leave. Outside the org: designing the learning journeys that turn first-time partners, hosts, or operators into experts at scale.

03

Orchestrate quality across the portfolio.

Connecting journeys across the entire service ecosystem so the experience standard holds at scale — no map lives alone, and the bar travels with it.

04

Ship AI tools, not just use them.

Custom agents in production, AI-supported workflows, behavioral models baked in — the infrastructure that helps teams ship AI-native work, not just consume off-the-shelf AI features.

05

Accelerate experimentation through AI.

Modeling actors, variables, and edge cases into AI I can run against — covering more ground, surfacing blind spots, and iterating before code is written. The experimentation layer that compresses weeks of synthesis into days.

06

Design for many actors, many variables.

A service touches VPs, PMs, frontline staff, partners, and end users — each with different jobs, constraints, and thresholds. I design for the whole ecosystem of actors, not the average user.
